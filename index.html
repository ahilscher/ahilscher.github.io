The Route

200 MILES ACROSS TEXAS

Gonzales to Houston. Wildflower hills, small towns with big hearts, a long night through the countryside, and a sunrise finish in the city.

The Starting Cannon

Gonzales

Every team gets a cannon boom and their own walk-up song. You run a loop together through downtown, past the "Come and Take It" monument, and then you're off.

Legs 3–10 · Rolling Hills

Shiner → Flatonia → Schulenburg

This is the stretch people photograph. Wildflowers everywhere, dirt roads, German-Czech towns with kolache shops. You'll pass Sam Houston's Tree, the actual oak where the general camped in 1836.

Legs 11–18 · The Heartland

Weimar → Columbus → Eagle Lake

The hills pick up around here. Legs 12 through 14 are the ones people warn you about. You'll cross the Colorado River right around sunset, which almost makes up for the elevation.

Legs 19–28 · The Night

Wallis → Fulshear → Katy

These towns are all asleep when you come through. It's dark, it's quiet, and everyone in the van is running on gas station coffee.

Legs 29–36 · The Finish

Memorial Park → Buffalo Bayou → Saint Arnold

You hit Houston's park trails as the sun comes up. The last leg runs through Memorial Park and along Buffalo Bayou with the skyline right there. Your whole team joins the final runner for the last stretch into the brewery.

The Experience

MORE THAN A RACE

Ask anyone who's done it. They'll talk about the race for maybe two minutes, then spend an hour on everything else.

The TIPP Party

Friday night in Gonzales. Pajamas required. Costumes encouraged.

Small Town Heart

Shiner. Flatonia. Schulenburg. Towns where folks put out lawn chairs at midnight to clap for runners they've never met.

The Night Legs

Somewhere between Eagle Lake and Wallis, it's just you, a headlamp, and more stars than you've seen in years.

Living History

You'll run past the oak where Sam Houston actually camped in 1836. You'll exchange wristbands under his statue in Hermann Park. The route is the real thing.

The Van Life

Twelve people. Two vans. Thirty-something hours. You'll somehow come out the other side as better friends.

The Finish

Your whole team runs the last stretch together into Saint Arnold Brewery. There's pizza. There's cold beer. There's the Houston skyline. Not a bad way to spend a Sunday morning.

How It Works

FROM SIGNUP TO FINISH LINE

Never done a relay? No problem. Here's how it works.

01

BUILD YOUR TEAM

Round up 4 to 12 people. Friends, coworkers, your running club, your family. Fast, slow, first-timers, all welcome.

02

REGISTER & PREP

Sign up, pick a team name, choose your walk-up song (yes, seriously), and divvy up the 36 legs among your runners.

03

RUN THE RELAY

One runner goes at a time. The rest of the team leapfrogs by van. Each person runs about 15–20 miles total, spread across 3 legs over the whole weekend.

04

FINISH TOGETHER

Your whole team runs the last stretch into Saint Arnold. Grab your medal. Grab a beer. Start arguing about next year's team name.
