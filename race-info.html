Be Prepared

WHAT TO BRING

Pack for running, sleeping in a van, and everything in between.

RUNNING GEAR

  • Running shoes (broken in, not brand new)
  • Multiple running outfits. You'll want to change between legs.
  • Reflective vest or gear (required for night legs)
  • Headlamp with fresh batteries (required for night legs)
  • Blinking rear safety light (required for night legs)
  • Hat or visor for sun protection
  • Body Glide or anti-chafe
  • Sunscreen

VAN ESSENTIALS

  • Sleeping bag or blanket for van naps
  • Pillow (you'll be grateful)
  • Baby wipes (showers are not really a thing)
  • Change of regular clothes
  • Towel
  • Deodorant (for the sake of your van-mates)
  • Flip flops or camp shoes
  • Phone charger and portable battery pack
  • Cash (small town gas stations)

FUEL

  • Water bottles or hydration pack
  • Electrolyte mix (Nuun, Liquid IV, etc.)
  • Energy gels or chews for your legs
  • Snacks - granola bars, trail mix, fruit
  • Cooler with ice for the van
  • Coffee supplies (or just count on gas stations)

TEAM STUFF

  • Printed leg maps and written directions (don't rely 100% on your phone)
  • Decorations for your van
  • Signs to cheer for your runner
  • Sharpie for signing each other's bibs
  • Camera
  • Bluetooth speaker for the van
  • Good attitude about sleeping in a van
Your Vehicle

VAN REQUIREMENTS

You provide the vans. TIR provides the route. Here's what you need to know.

THE BASICS

Most teams use two 12-passenger or 15-passenger rental vans. You split your team of 12 into two groups of 6. Van 1 runs a set of legs, then rests while Van 2 takes over. The vans leapfrog along the route, meeting your current runner at each exchange point.

Some teams run with one van and fewer people. Some teams have brought an RV as a mobile base camp. As long as you can get your runners to the exchange points safely, you have flexibility.

VAN SAFETY RULES

  • Vans must have working headlights, taillights, and hazard lights. No exceptions.
  • When following your runner at night, use hazard lights and maintain a safe distance behind. Do not blind the runner or oncoming traffic.
  • Vans must pull completely off the road at exchange points. Do not block traffic or park on curves.
  • Designated drivers should not be running legs immediately before or after driving. Sleep-deprived driving is the biggest safety risk at any relay.
  • No alcohol consumption in the van during the relay. Save it for Saint Arnold.
  • Keep the van stocked with water, first aid supplies, and emergency contact info.
  • If a runner feels unsafe on any leg (traffic, weather, injury), the van should pick them up. The race isn't worth a trip to the ER.
Stay Safe

SAFETY & RACE RULES

TIR has been running since 2008 with an excellent safety record. Let's keep it that way.

NIGHT RUNNING (REQUIRED)

Night legs are roughly Legs 20–30, depending on your team's start time. For any leg running between sunset and sunrise, every runner is required to have a reflective vest, a front-facing headlamp, and a rear-facing blinking red light. Your van should follow behind with hazard lights on.

These aren't suggestions. Teams not in compliance may be asked to stop until the runner is properly equipped.

MEDICAL & EMERGENCIES

There is no medical staff on the course between exchange points. Each team is responsible for their own first aid. Bring a basic kit (bandages, pain relief, ice packs, blister care). If a runner needs medical attention, call 911 first, then notify race organizers.

Report any safety concerns or course hazards using the Safety Enhancement Form or call Jay at 512-716-5041.

RELAY EXCHANGE RULES

Runners exchange a wristband at designated exchange points. The incoming runner must hand the wristband to the outgoing runner — no passing it through van windows or skipping ahead. Exchange points have specific operating hours, so plan your pace accordingly. Your team captain should have the exchange coordinates spreadsheet.

ROAD RULES

Runners must stay on the shoulder or as far to the left side of the road as safely possible (facing traffic). Obey all traffic signals. Do not run on private property. On shoulderless bridges, your van may escort you — race rules allow this.

This is a USATF-sanctioned event. Standard USATF road racing rules apply. The full rulebook is available from the race directors.

WEATHER

Late March in central Texas can be anything from 40°F mornings to 85°F afternoons. Rain is possible. Pack for layers. Night legs will be colder than you think, especially between Eagle Lake and Wallis when you're tired and it's 3 AM.

WAIVERS

All participants must sign a waiver. Runners under 18 need a parent/guardian-signed minor waiver (PDF). Bring signed copies to the start.
