Questions?

FREQUENTLY ASKED

Your team entry, bibs, a team wristband for relay exchanges, access to all exchange points and rest stops, the finish line celebration at Saint Arnold Brewery (pizza and beer included), finisher medals, and the Friday night TIPP party. You'll also get access to the TIR app with live tracking.

Teams can have 4 to 12 runners. Most teams do 10-12 split across two vans, with each person running about 15-20 miles total over three legs. Smaller teams are absolutely fine — you'll just each run more legs. Some teams have done it with as few as 6.

Register as a Free Agent. We'll connect you with teams looking for extra runners. It's a great way to meet people and get your first relay experience.

If one person pays the team lump sum ($2,050 at current pricing), all 12 team members register for free afterward. If you pay individually, the captain pays $185 and each additional member pays $205. For a full team of 12, individual pricing comes out to about $2,440 total, so the lump sum saves you a few hundred bucks.

Yes. Most teams rent two large passenger vans (12 or 15 passenger). You'll need vehicles to leapfrog along the course, following your runners and meeting them at each exchange point. Check the Race Info page for full van requirements and safety rules.

If you can run 3 to 7 miles, you can do this. Leg distances range from about 3.7 to 6.8 miles, and each person runs 3 legs spread over the whole weekend with hours of rest in between. Plenty of walkers and walk-runners do TIR every year.
