Leg by Leg

THE FULL BREAKDOWN

Difficulty is ranked 1 (hardest) to 36 (easiest), based on distance, elevation change, and turns. Category 4 legs are the toughest. Category 1 is the most forgiving.

01

GONZALES

PROLOGUE + LEGS 1–2 · MILES 0–10.6

Your whole team runs the 1.15-mile prologue loop through downtown Gonzales together — past the courthouse, past the "Come and Take It" monument. Then the relay begins. The first legs take you southeast out of town toward Shiner.

LegDistCatSummary
P1.15—Tour historic Gonzales, going around perhaps the best courthouse in all of Texas.
14.07CAT 3Leave Gonzales from the Memorial Museum.
25.34CAT 2Straight down Hwy 90A towards Shiner, finishing at Sam Houston's Oak.
02

SHINER → FLATONIA → SCHULENBURG

LEGS 3–13 · MILES 10.6–61.6

The stretch people photograph. Dirt roads through wildflower country, German-Czech towns with painted churches, and the actual oak where Sam Houston camped in 1836. Flatonia's Lyric Theater and Praha's St. Mary's Church are highlights.

34.86CAT 3Excellent dirt road running, finishing atop a notable hill. Arguably the most rural part of the course.
44.08CAT 2Continues on peaceful dirt roads before finishing on the outskirts of Shiner.

64.10CAT 1Leave Shiner and run back out to the Texas countryside.
73.71CAT 1Panoramic views of Texas hillsides. Shortest leg on the course.
85.18CAT 3Dirt roads and country pavement, finishing in Old Moulton.
94.68CAT 2Starts in Old Moulton, has a bit of dirt roads and finishes after a decent climb.

126.13CAT 4Picturesque countryside leg, finishing on Wolters.
136.05CAT 4Countryside run under train tracks. Runners exercise extra caution to cross a short bridge. Finish in downtown Schulenburg.
03

WEIMAR → COLUMBUS → EAGLE LAKE

LEGS 14–23 · MILES 61.6–117.7

The hills pick up. Legs 14, 16, and 17 are the ones people warn you about — all Category 4, all over 6 miles. You'll cross the Colorado River Bridge right around sunset. By Eagle Lake, the terrain flattens out and night is coming.

04

WALLIS → FULSHEAR → KATY

LEGS 24–30 · MILES 117.7–154.1

These towns are asleep when you come through. It's dark, it's quiet, and everyone in the van is running on gas station coffee. You'll cross the Brazos River on Leg 27. By Leg 30, you're hitting Katy and Good Times Running Company throws a party to welcome you.

275.70CAT 3Straight up Simonton Road, over the Brazos River, finishing at Anthonie's in Simonton.

05

MEMORIAL PARK → BUFFALO BAYOU → SAINT ARNOLD

LEGS 31–36 · MILES 154.1–~200

You hit Houston's park trails as the sun comes up. George Bush Park, Terry Hershey Trail, Memorial Park — some of the city's best running. The final leg takes you along Buffalo Bayou with the skyline right there. Your whole team joins the last runner for the finish into Saint Arnold Brewery.

LegDistCatSummary
315.32CAT 2Into George Bush Park, over a quarter-mile boardwalk near the Barker Reservoir.
325.38CAT 2George Bush Park to Terry Hershey trail — well-shaded and scenic.
335.91CAT 4Hike and bike trails into Memorial Park. The running hub of Houston.
345.15CAT 3Through Memorial Park and out along the Bayou toward downtown.
355.72CAT 3Buffalo Bayou Park with the Houston skyline in view the whole way.
364.19CAT 2The final leg. Around downtown's north side to Saint Arnold Brewing Company. Your team joins you for the last stretch.
Rest Stops

KEY EXCHANGE POINTS

These are the spots where your vans will swap runners. Some have amenities, some are just a parking lot under the stars.

Exchange 5 · Mile 23

Downtown Shiner

Home of Shiner beer. Gas stations, restaurants, and real bathrooms. Teams tend to linger here.

Exchange 10 · Mile 45

Flatonia · Lyric Theater

Downtown exchange with food options. The Lyric Theater is the backdrop.

Exchange 13 · Mile 62

Downtown Schulenburg

Look for the painted churches nearby. Good spot for a van crew meal.

Exchange 15 · Mile 73

Downtown Weimar

Music and entertainment at the exchange. Energy picks up here.

Exchange 18 · Mile 91

Columbus · Snappy's

Just south of I-10. Gas, food, and a real rest stop feel.

Exchange 25 · Mile 127

Wallis · Brandt 1910

Wedding venue turned rest stop. Some teams get a few hours of indoor sleep here.

Exchange 30 · Mile 154

Katy · Good Times Running Co.

The Good Mornin' Party. Everyone stops here. Coffee, cheering, and a second wind.

Exchange 35 · Mile ~190

Memorial Park

Houston's running hub. You're almost there. One more leg to go.

Finish · Mile ~200

Saint Arnold Brewery

Pizza. Cold beer. Medals. The Houston skyline. You did it.
